The black pod disease is caused mainly by Phytophthora palmivora and Phytophthora megakarya. These belong to the large group of plant damaging oomycetes and they infect every living tissue of the cocoa plant.

Fungicides are widely used for controlling the black pod disease. The fungicides inhibit the mycelia growth.

The Cocoa Swollen Shoot Virus Disease (CSSVD) is mainly transmitted by the feeding actions of several mealybug species. The mealybugs feed on all parts of the cocoa tree including flowers, cherells, pods and leaves.

The only cure for the CSSVD is to cut out visibly infected trees. The official eradication campaign in Ghana has cut out more than 200 million trees since 1946.

The most effective way to get rid of pests is through regular complete harvesting of the pods, enforcing a break in pod production and applying insecticides such as organochlorines, organophosphates and pyrethroids.
